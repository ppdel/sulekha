Agriculture in Nepal

Photo Credits: Jamuna Risal Paudel

Main Crops Grown in Nepal

The main crops that are grown in Nepal are rice, corn, wheat and millet1. These are all staples to life in Nepal. Rice is an irreplaceable element to Nepali cuisine, where it features in a lot of Nepali foods due to its ease of access and its adaptability to any dish. Rice is something that many Nepali people are very thankful for, due to the fact that it contributes to almost 50% of all cereal based calorie intakes2 for many people, where it is regularly consumed at least once or twice a day.

Agriculture to Nepali people.

For many in Nepal, agriculture is a lifestyle. About 70% of the population lives on subsistence farms, where they cultivate just enough for themselves and their families. Most of the subsistence farmers and farms rely on old-fashioned farming (without modern equipment) due to unavailability of proper equipment, such as tractors.3 This creates a heavy reliance on one’s self to carry out all the hard manual labour work, including plowing, fertilizing, etc. Also, due to the lack of insecticides and pesticides being used to protect one’s plants there is a risk of crops being damaged and thrown away, which can make a big difference for a poor farmer in Nepal.

Who is reliant on agriculture?

To Nepali people, agriculture is a subsistence practice. People cultivate only to provide for themselves and their close ones. Only about 10%4 of farmers actually sell their produce for a living. Most people who are reliant on farming did not get the opportunity to go to school and get educated, because of things like poverty, affecting about two-fifths of the population5. This caused those who are educated to migrate out of the country for foreign jobs, where they can make more money and have further opportunities for improving their quality of life.
